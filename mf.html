What are Mutual Funds?

A mutual fund is a professionally managed investment mechanism that pools capital from numerous investors. This collective corpus is then invested in a diversified portfolio of securities, such as stocks, bonds, money market instruments, and other assets, in accordance with a pre-defined investment objective. This pooling approach allows individual investors, even with small amounts of money, to gain access to a wide range of securities and sectors. The core principle is diversification, which helps mitigate risk. All mutual funds in India are regulated by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI), ensuring a fair and transparent system.
